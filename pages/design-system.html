01 — Philosophy

The Name

השם

Odysee is a respelling of Odyssey — the long journey home. The original is Homer's. The respelling is ours.

Removing the second 'y' separates the product from the literary reference and grants the name its own visual identity. It reads quietly — not a brand demanding attention, but a word that suggests motion and patience. The kind of name that earns its meaning over time, the way a well-worn passport does.

Odyssey ↓ Odysee Lose a 'y'. Find a brand.

The mark sits where the 'O' should be — a roundel containing a dotted trail. The brand and the word are the same thing: [roundel]dysee. Travel is built from discrete steps, not from a single unbroken line, and the name carries that idea before you've read a word of copy.

Always written in Latin script — even within Hebrew text. Never transliterated. Capital O, lowercase rest. Tico follows the same rule.

02 — Philosophy

The Mark

הסמל

A circle, a trail, two points. The roundel is the world. The trail is the journey across it.

Twenty-four short dashes arc through 48 degrees of the lower hemisphere, rotated −32° so the path reads as motion — not a smile. The start point is navy, small and certain: where you are. The end point is terracotta, larger and warmer: where you're going. The arc is dotted because travel is built from intervals — a flight, a city, a meal, a morning — not a continuous line.

Roundel
r = 45.4, stroke 9.2. White interior so the trail reads against any surface — page, video, sticker.
Trail Arc
M 26 50 A 24 24 0 0 0 74 50. Dasharray 0.1 12 = round caps with wide gaps. Rotated −32° so it reads as motion, not a smile.
Start · Navy
r = 3.6. Small, certain. The pinpoint of here.
Destination · Terracotta
r = 5.4. Larger, warmer. The gravity of there — the place pulling the journey forward.

The wordmark replaces the 'O' of Odysee with the roundel itself. The brand mark and the word are not separate elements that sit beside each other — they share a glyph. There is no version of the name without the mark.

03 — Philosophy

The Palette

הפלטה

Navy. Terracotta. Ivory. Night sky over warm earth, with the paper that holds them.

The combination is older than design — it's how almost every culture has rendered the contrast between the technological and the human. Navy is the system: precise, dark, cool. Terracotta is the traveler: warm, human, slightly imperfect. Ivory is the page that lets both speak.

Navy
#0C1F3F
The system. Precise, dark, cool. Carries the logo at rest, anchors dark surfaces, frames the work.
Terracotta
#B85A3E
The traveler. Warm, human, slightly imperfect. Used for action, accent, the destination cap — never for body text.
Ivory
#F6F4EF
The page. Light, warm, restful. Not white — paper. Default surface in light mode, text on dark surfaces.

Around these three sit a quiet family of mist neutrals (50→900) for surfaces and text, and four signal accents — teal for interaction and AI, purple for design states, amber for warnings, red for errors. The signals appear sparingly. The brand is the three.

04 — Philosophy

The Voice — Tico

הקול של Tico

Tico — the persona of Odysee — speaks like a friend who has traveled a lot. Not like a search engine. Not like a call center.

Five attributes define the tone. Each is a constraint, not a flavor. If a sentence violates any of them, it's not Tico — even if it's technically correct.

חכם
Smart — Offers depth without being asked
Yes"טוקיו עובד הכי טוב בסוף אוקטובר — הקהל דועך והעלווה בשיא."
No"טוקיו היא עיר נפלאה עם הרבה אפשרויות מעניינות!"
חם
Warm — Natural language, never jargon
Yes"שמתי לב שאתה מעדיף מקומות שקטים — תדלג על Tsukiji בשעת פתיחה."
No"בהתאם להעדפות שהוזנו, האתרים הבאים מומלצים."
בטוח
Confident — Recommends, doesn't suggest
Yes"קח את Shinkansen ב-7 בבוקר — שווה להתחיל מוקדם."
No"אולי כדאי לשקול לקחת רכבת מוקדמת, אם אפשרי."
תמציתי
Concise — Says what's needed, stops
Yes"טיסה עוכבה ב-40 דקות. הזזתי ארוחת הערב שלך."
No"זיהינו עדכון לוח זמנים. יש לעיין במידע הבא..."
אישי
Personal — References what he knows
Yes"אהבת את הראמן באוסקה — יש מקום דומה ליד המלון."
No"הנה מסעדות פופולריות ליד מיקומך."

05 — Philosophy

Design Principles

עקרונות עיצוב

Five rules that drive every decision in the system. They exist so we can move fast without re-deciding the basics.

01
No shadows.
Depth comes from contrast and a 1px border. Never from a glow or a drop shadow. The system reads flat and intentional, not glassy.
02
Intentional motion.
Every transition has a job: confirm an action, indicate state, help orient. If removing an animation doesn't hurt comprehension, the animation doesn't belong.
03
The 8px grid.
Every space — gap, padding, margin — is a multiple of 8. This isn't OCD; it's the difference between a layout that feels deliberate and one that feels arbitrary.
04
Hebrew is first-class.
RTL by default for Hebrew content. Heebo paired with Sora for Latin. No transliteration. Tico and Odysee are always written in Latin script regardless of surrounding language.
05
Three themes, one system.
Light, Dark, System. Tokens drive every color; switching themes flips a single attribute on the root element. The brand identity is invariant.
